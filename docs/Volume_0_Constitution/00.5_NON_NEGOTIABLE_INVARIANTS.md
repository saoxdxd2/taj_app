# 00.5_NON_NEGOTIABLE_INVARIANTS.md

# Project Laws (PLI)

Version: 1.0

Status: ABSOLUTE

Priority: Highest

These rules override every other engineering document.

If any implementation conflicts with these laws, the implementation is wrong.

---

# Purpose

This document defines the immutable laws governing the TAJ FROID ERP.

Unlike coding standards or architecture guidelines, these are absolute invariants.

They are never violated.

They are never bypassed.

They are never ignored for convenience.

---

# Law 1 — Business Before Software

The software serves the business.

The business never adapts itself to satisfy software limitations.

---

# Law 2 — Database Is The Single Source Of Truth

Only the database owns business truth.

No PDF.

No Excel.

No OCR.

No Cache.

No Report.

No UI.

No AI.

May replace the database as the authoritative source.

---

# Law 3 — History Is Sacred

Business history is never destroyed.

Invoices.

Payments.

Purchases.

Expenses.

Journal entries.

Audit events.

Remain permanently traceable.

---

# Law 4 — Audit Everything Important

Every critical business action creates an immutable audit event.

Silent changes are forbidden.

---

# Law 5 — Never Lose Money

Every financial calculation must be reproducible.

Every report must be explainable.

No hidden calculations.

No undocumented formulas.

---

# Law 6 — Stock Cannot Become Impossible

Inventory can never become negative.

Inventory movements must always be explainable.

Every stock modification has a reason.

---

# Law 7 — Business Rules Exist Only Once

Business logic exists in exactly one location.

Duplication is forbidden.

---

# Law 8 — UI Never Owns Business Logic

Windows.

Dialogs.

Widgets.

Tables.

Pages.

Never calculate business rules.

---

# Law 9 — OCR Never Bypasses Validation

OCR accelerates data entry.

OCR never inserts trusted data.

Every OCR result passes through the normal validation pipeline.

---

# Law 10 — AI Never Makes Final Business Decisions

Artificial Intelligence may suggest.

Never decide.

The human operator always validates business-critical actions.

---

# Law 11 — Everything Important Is Recoverable

Backups.

Configurations.

Documents.

Database.

Must always be recoverable.

Recovery must be tested.

---

# Law 12 — No Silent Failure

Errors must be:

Detected.

Logged.

Explained.

Audited when appropriate.

---

# Law 13 — Permissions Are Enforced Everywhere

The UI.

Business services.

Repositories.

Reports.

Administration.

Must all respect permissions.

Never rely on hidden buttons alone.

---

# Law 14 — Every Business Entity Has One Owner

No shared ownership.

No duplicated responsibilities.

Ownership is explicit.

---

# Law 15 — Architecture Cannot Drift

Architectural changes require an Engineering Decision Record (EDR).

No ad-hoc redesigns.

---

# Law 16 — Reuse Before Reinvention

Before implementing anything ask:

Does Python already solve this?

Does a mature library solve this?

Can existing project code solve this?

If yes, reuse first.

---

# Law 17 — Performance Never Sacrifices Correctness

Correct.

↓

Reliable.

↓

Maintainable.

↓

Fast.

Never reverse this order.

---

# Law 18 — The User Must Trust The Software

Every number displayed by the ERP must be explainable.

No "magic" values.

No unexplained totals.

No hidden adjustments.

---

# Law 19 — Documentation Evolves With The Code

Code changes.

Documentation changes.

Architecture changes.

Documentation changes.

Database changes.

Documentation changes.

Never separate them.

---

# Law 20 — The ERP Must Still Be Understandable In Ten Years

Every engineering decision should optimize for long-term clarity rather than short-term speed.

If future engineers cannot understand today's work, today's work is incomplete.

---

# Final Principle

These laws define the identity of the project.

Languages may change.

Frameworks may change.

Libraries may change.

Operating systems may change.

The database may change.

But these laws remain constant.
